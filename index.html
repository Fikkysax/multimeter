    Troubleshoot Electrical Faults (Practical Checklist)

    Problem: Device not turning on

    • Step 1: Measure supply voltage (DCV for batteries/adapters, ACV for mains).
    • Step 2: Check fuse / plug / power cable using continuity mode.
    • Step 3: Check switch continuity (ON should beep; OFF should not).
    • Step 4: Check for broken wires, loose joints, or burnt connectors.
    If voltage is present but device is dead, the fault is likely inside (broken connection, switch, fuse, internal component).

    Problem: Short circuit / breaker trips

    • Power OFF. Use resistance/continuity to test between live & neutral (or + and ‚àí).
    • If it beeps strongly / near 0Œ©, you likely have a short.
    • Disconnect parts one by one to find where the short disappears.
    • Look for melted insulation, moisture, or burnt components.
    Shorts on mains are dangerous. If you‚Äôre unsure, use a qualified electrician for 220‚Äì240V circuits.

    Problem: Battery drains fast

    • Measure battery voltage (DCV). Compare to expected value (e.g., AA ~1.5V new, 9V battery ~9V).
    • Measure current draw by inserting meter in series (start on 10A port if unsure).
    • High current draw when ‚Äúoff‚Äù can mean a leakage path or faulty component.

    Problem: Wrong readings

    • Confirm the dial mode: DC vs AC matters.
    • Confirm red lead is in the correct port (VŒ©mA vs 10A).
    • Replace meter battery if display is dim or unstable.
    • Check current fuse inside the meter if current always reads 0.
